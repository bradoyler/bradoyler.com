AI News Digest - November 2025

Published: Nov 30, 2025 •
#blog #ai

Here’s a roundup of some of the most notable AI developments from the past month, focusing on breakthroughs, policy shifts, and industry moves. I've pulled from reliable sources to keep it fresh and factual.

1. U.S. Launches "Genesis Mission" for AI-Driven Science

The White House issued an executive order on November 25 kicking off the Genesis Mission, a national initiative to integrate AI across federal research facilities. It aims to double R&D productivity by linking data, computing, and labs into a "closed-loop system" for tackling challenges in energy, biotech, quantum tech, and more. The Department of Energy will prioritize 20 key problems within 60 days, with early focuses on nuclear energy and national security. This builds on earlier 2025 EOs removing AI barriers and speeding data center permits.

2. Anthropic Releases Claude Opus 4.5, Sets New Coding Benchmarks

On November 25, Anthropic unveiled Claude Opus 4.5, a frontier model excelling in coding tasks and outperforming rivals like GPT-4o on benchmarks. It also introduced "Petri," an AI tool for automating safety audits in model development. This ramps up competition in the "frontier AI" race, with early tests showing stronger reasoning for complex software engineering.

3. MIT Study: AI Could Replace 11.7% of U.S. Jobs Now

A November 26 MIT report, using the Iceberg Index simulation, estimates AI can already automate 11.7% of U.S. roles in finance, healthcare, and professional services—equivalent to about 18 million jobs. The tool maps disruptions by zip code, urging policymakers to invest in reskilling. It's a wake-up call on AI's immediate economic impact, with calls for targeted training programs.

4. Google Unveils "Hope" Architecture for Lifelong Learning AI

Google Research dropped "Hope," a self-modifying ML paradigm on November 7, treating models as nested problem stacks to learn new skills without forgetting old ones—mimicking brain-like evolution. It beat transformers on language, reasoning, and continual learning tests, marking a shift from static models to adaptive ones.

5. Regulatory Shifts: EU Deregulates AI, U.S. Goes Further; Virginia Eyes Minors' Access

On November 25, the EU announced deregulation to boost AI growth, while the U.S. pushes even lighter oversight amid Nvidia's strong earnings staving off bubble fears. Separately, Virginia proposed laws on November 27 to restrict AI chatbots for minors, citing safety risks like inappropriate content. TikTok countered with new AI content controls and a $2M literacy fund the same day.

6. OpenAI's ChatGPT Adds Shopping Research; Alibaba's Qwen Glasses Launch

OpenAI integrated a "shopping research" feature into ChatGPT on November 25, letting it compare products and generate reports—though it's bowing out of medical/legal advice due to regs. Alibaba followed with Qwen AI glasses on November 27, blending Qwen LLM into wearable tech for real-time assistance, and rebranded its Tongyi chatbot as the Qwen App for consumer use.

7. Other Quick Hits

  • DeepSeek Math-V2: China's new model challenges Gemini 3 on math benchmarks (Nov 29).
  • Swedish AI for Dementia Detection: Örebro University’s EEG-analyzing systems distinguish dementia types with explainable AI (Nov 27).
  • Hybrid AI Trend: Blending logic systems with LLMs for human-level intelligence gains traction (Nov 25).
  • Nvidia Faces Competition: Google TPUs expand deals, hitting Nvidia stock (Nov 29).

AI's moving fast—regulations are loosening for innovation, but ethical/job concerns are ramping up. If you want deeper dives on any of these, let me know!
